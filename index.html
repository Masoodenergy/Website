The demand for energy
is growing in the developing world.

In developing economies, the grid is not a reliable source of energy, owing to very high energy demand and a lower supply of electricity. Developing economies have a high population growth rate and an emerging middle class.

The grid in the developing world
fails to adequately meet the demand for electricity.

Since big power projects are centrally planned by governments, even with favourable estimates the utility companies often fail to build power plants fast enough to meet the growing user demand. A big hurdle to the development of energy projects is low access to capital for developing economies. When these issues mix with political instability and corruption, it means projects get delayed, cancelled or fail to be executed in time to meet the demand.

Renewable sources of energy are very well suited to meet the growing energy needs in the developing world because they allow people to meet their energy needs without the unreliable and unstable grid. For citizens of developing countries to live off the grid completely and meet all their needs, they would need to be able to store the excess energy produced from residential solar panels or other sources of renewable energy. They must also be able to use power at night and during peak times when it is needed most.The GDP of most developing countries would grow just by securing access to reliable energy that can meet demands of residential and commercial users around the clock. Energy storage can play a key role in the economic development of the developing countries.

We are developing energy storage technology that allows users to store energy more economically and efficiently.
